# THE PROBLEM

## Builders Have the Tools. They Still Build the Wrong Things.

The cost of building software has collapsed. A solo founder in 2026 can ship a product in days. The infrastructure barrier is gone. The market validation barrier is not.

Most products fail before they find a customer. Not because the technology failed, but because the idea was never confirmed. Founders build from intuition, skip the uncomfortable work of proving demand, and discover months later that nobody wanted what they built.

Confirming that a market opportunity exists requires searching across a dozen platforms, reading hundreds of community posts, mapping competitors, scoring the gap against the right criteria, and making a go or no-go decision without letting optimism distort the evidence.

Done properly, this takes **two to three days of focused work**. In practice, most founders skip it entirely or do it poorly in an hour.

Even founders who validate well still face execution complexity. Research, product development, coding, distribution, content, customer acquisition, operations, and financial tracking are all separate disciplines.

Building a company that handles all of them requires a team.

**ARIA is that team.**

The problem is not that founders cannot build.  
It is that they build the wrong things, too slowly, one at a time, without the full range of skills a company needs to survive past its first product.

# THE METHODOLOGY

## Five Stages. No Exceptions.

Every project follows the same pipeline.

---

## P01 - Signal Detection (48 hours)

Ideas enter from two sources:

1. **Community sourced**
2. **ARIA generated**

Every idea must pass three signals:

| Signal | Description |
|---|---|
| Pain | Real emotional complaints from users |
| Spend | Evidence people are already paying |
| Gap | A clear weakness in existing solutions |

### Scoring

Each idea is scored on a **25-point rubric** across:

- Pain
- Spend
- Speed to Ship
- Reach
- Monetization Clarity

| Score | Decision |
|---|---|
| 20+ | Ship immediately |
| 14–19 | Additional research |
| <14 | Kill |

---

## P02 - Validation (72 hours)

ARIA:

1. Builds a landing page
2. Runs distribution tests
3. Tracks organic signups

**Threshold to proceed:**

- 5 organic signups from strangers

Two failed tests = **kill the project**

---

## Build - 7 days

ARIA builds only what the validation promised.

Principles:

- One feature
- One audience
- One price

Stripe is connected **before launch**.

---

## Launch - 2 weeks

ARIA produces the complete distribution strategy:

- Community posts
- Email sequences
- DM outreach
- Follow-up campaigns

Target:

- **$100 MRR OR**
- **20 active users**

---

## Scale or Kill

Projects either:

- **Scale**
- **Die**

Kill criteria:

- Flat or declining revenue for two weeks

ARIA logs learnings and immediately begins the next opportunity.
